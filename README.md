## <center>Replicate it till you make it</center>

### What's this repo about?

This repository is my attempt to learn real Machine Learning and Software Engineering by studying and re-implementing projects from top engineers and researchers.

In the era of LLMs and AI agents, it has become very easy to generate working code, project structures, and entire systems with just a few prompts. While this is powerful, it can also lead to a dangerous habit, especially for people who are still learning, like me.

Even when we try to read that generated code, something often feels missing.

I believe that missing piece is **struggle**.

Struggle is a fundamental part of learning. When we remove it entirely, we also remove much of the deep understanding that comes from working through hard problems ourselves.

Another belief that motivates this project is simple:

> If you want to write/understand high-quality software, you need to read high-quality software written by great engineers.

With that in mind, this repository is an attempt to learn directly from real-world projects.

For each project, I will:

* Study the original repository
* Understand its architecture and design decisions
* Analyze the engineering patterns used
* Reimplement the project (or its core ideas) by hand

The goal is not to copy the code, but to **reconstruct the thinking behind it**.

As Cal Newport wrote in his book *Deep Work*:

> “Two core abilities for thriving in the new economy:
>
> 1. The ability to quickly master hard things
> 2. The ability to produce at an elite level, in terms of both quality and speed.”

Through this project, I hope to develop at least one of them.

---

**P.S.**
I do use AI tools in my workflow. The goal here is not to reject them. Instead, I try to **struggle first**, and only use AI afterward when I am stuck or when I want to validate my understanding.

Frankly enough, I used ChatGPT to refine this README.md file.
